2. Brief History
‚ÄĘ
In the 1980s, the emergence of large-scale lexical resources enabled the 
switch from hand-coded knowledge to automatic knowledge extraction 
from these resources
‚ÄĘ
In the 1990s, knowledge-based solutions helped improve of WSD
‚Äď Corpus-based techniques were picking up
‚ÄĘ
In the 2000s, knowledge-based and supervised learning solutions reached 
human-like accuracy levels, and other methods emerged such as domain 
adaptation, semi-supervised, and unsupervised word embedding solutions
‚ÄĘ
Hybrid systems are currently trending, combining knowledge-based and 
corpus-based techniques

4.1. Selecting Words for Disambiguation
‚ÄĘ
Two methods to select target words for disambiguation:
‚Äď All-words: the system is expected to disambiguate all words in a (flat) 
textual document
‚ÄĘ Although considered as a complete and exhaustive approach, yet the all-
words approach remains extremely time-consuming
‚Äď Lexical-sample: specific target words are selected for disambiguation 
(usually one word per sentence) 
‚ÄĘ Often the most ambiguous words, chosen using supervised learning 
methods trained to recognize salient words in sentences
‚Äď More useful and efficient in practice

4.2. Identifying and Representing Context
‚ÄĘ
WSD relies on the notion of context such that words that appear together 
in the same context usually have related meanings
‚ÄĘ
The context of a word in traditional flat textual data usually consists of the 
set of terms in the word‚Äôs vicinity
‚Äď Terms occurring to the left and right of the considered word, within a certain 
predefined window size
‚ÄĘ
Other features can also be used to describe context, such as information 
resulting from linguistic pre-processing:
‚Äď
Part-of-speech tags (e.g., verb, subject, etc.), grammatical relationships (e.g., verb-
subject, verb-object, etc.)

‚Äď Another direction to improve XML semantic analysis and disambiguation is to

‚ÄĘ This is different from conventional concept-based semantic analysis, which utilizes explicit
